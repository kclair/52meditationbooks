52 Meditation Books

In which I attempt to read a meditation book a week for one year.

Start Where You Are

Pema Chödrön

One of the most powerful teachings of the Buddhist tradition is that as long as you are wishing for things to change, they never will. As long as you're wanting yourself to get better, you won't. As long as you have an orientation toward the future, you can never just relax into what you already have or already are.


Bodhichitta has three qualities: (1) it is soft and gentle, which is compassion; (2) at the same time, it is clear and sharp, which is called prajna; and (3) it is open. This last quality of Boddhichitta is called shunyata and is also known as emptiness. Emptiness sounds cold. However, boddhichitta isn't cold at all, because there's a heart quality -- the warmth of compassion that pervades the space and clarity.


Slogan: "In post meditation, be a child of illusion." Being a child of illusion also has to do with not being a walking battleground. We have such strong feelings of good and evil, right and wrong ... the truth is that good and bad coexist, sour and sweet coexist. They aren't opposed to each other. We could start to open our eyes and our hearts to that deep way of perceiving, like moving into a whole new dimension of experience.


Strangely enough, we blame others and put so much energy into the object of anger or whatever it is because we're afraid that this anger or sorrow or loneliness is going to last forever. Therefor, instead of relating directly with the sorrow or the loneliness or the anger, we think the way to end it is to blame it on somebody else .... Instead, acting it out is what makes it last.


The main point of "Be grateful to everyone" - the dig - is that you want to get rid of the situations that make you the most crazy ... this slogan encourages you to realize that when you've met your match you've found a teacher.


I was once instructed to meditate on thoughts. I investigated the nature of thought for two whole months. I can tell you firsthand that you can never find a thought. There is nothing there of substance, but with our minds we make it Extremely Big Deal.


What is meant by neurosis is that in limitless, timeless space - with which we could connect at any time - we continually have tunnel vision and lock ourselves into a room and put bolts on the door.


Reproach can be very powerful. You yourself teach yourself the dharma in your own words. You can teach yourself the four noble truths, you can teach yourself about taking refuge - anything that has to do with that moment when you're just about to recreate samsara as if you personally had invented it. Look ahead to the rest of your life and ask yourself what you want it to add up to.


The key to feeling at home with your body, mind, and emotions, to feeling worthy to live on this planet, comes from being able to lighten up.


When one of the emperors of China asked Boddhidharma what enlightenment was his answer was "Lots of space, nothing holy."
